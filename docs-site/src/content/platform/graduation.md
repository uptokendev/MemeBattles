Graduation is the transition from bonding-curve launch to post-graduation liquidity.

The graduation threshold is **50 BNB**.

## What happens at graduation

When a campaign graduates:

1. The campaign reaches the 50 BNB threshold.
2. A **2% finalize fee** is taken from raised liquidity before LP.
3. The remaining amount is split.
4. **80%** goes to LP liquidity.
5. **20%** goes to the creator payout.
6. The campaign enters the DEX stage.

## Minimum graduation example

At the 50 BNB threshold:

| Step | Amount |
| --- | ---: |
| Raised liquidity | 50 BNB |
| Finalize fee, 2% | 1 BNB |
| Remaining after fee | 49 BNB |
| LP liquidity, 80% of remaining | 39.2 BNB |
| Creator payout, 20% of remaining | 9.8 BNB |

This is why graduation matters for creators. A successful graduation creates a direct creator payout while also deploying liquidity.

## Why this structure exists

Graduation should reward creators without draining the entire launch.

The 80/20 split is designed to:

- give the token real liquidity after graduation
- reward creators for successful campaign execution
- keep economics predictable
- avoid unclear manual payout negotiations

## After graduation

After graduation, the campaign is no longer only a bonding-curve launch. It becomes a post-graduation market with LP liquidity and broader trading behavior.
